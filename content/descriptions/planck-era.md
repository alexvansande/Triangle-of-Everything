The earliest conceivable epoch of the universe, lasting from $t = 0$ to $t \approx 10^{-43}$ seconds (the [[Planck Time]]). During this era, the four fundamental forces — gravity, the strong force, the weak force, and electromagnetism — are thought to have been unified into a single interaction. The temperature exceeds $10^{32}$ K and energy densities surpass the [[Planck Energy]], meaning general relativity and quantum mechanics cannot be treated separately. No current physical theory can describe this epoch; a complete theory of quantum gravity is required. The Planck era ends when gravity separates from the other three forces, marking the transition to the [[Grand Unified Theory Era|GRAND UNIFIED THEORY ERA]].
