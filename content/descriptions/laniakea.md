The Laniakea Supercluster is our home supercluster, spanning approximately 500 million light-years and containing roughly 100,000 galaxies including the Milky Way. Its name means "immense heaven" in Hawaiian, and it was defined in 2014 by mapping the peculiar velocities of galaxies. Within Laniakea, all galaxies are flowing toward the "Great Attractor," a gravitational anomaly in the direction of the constellation Norma.
