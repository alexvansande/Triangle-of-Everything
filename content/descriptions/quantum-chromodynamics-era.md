The epoch from $\sim 10^{-12}$ s to $\sim 10^{-6}$ s after the [[Big Bang]], also known as the quark epoch. All four fundamental forces now operate independently. The universe is a quark-gluon plasma — a sea of free quarks, gluons, and leptons at temperatures above $\sim 10^{12}$ K. As the universe cools below the QCD confinement scale ($\sim 150$ MeV), quarks can no longer exist freely and become permanently bound into hadrons — [[Proton & Neutron|protons, neutrons]], and other composite particles — in a process called hadronization. This transition is studied experimentally in heavy-ion collisions at facilities like the [[Large Hadron Collider]] and RHIC, which briefly recreate the quark-gluon plasma. The era ends when the universe has cooled enough that hadrons dominate, setting the stage for [[Big Bang Nucleosynthesis|NUCLEOSYNTHESIS]].
