The world's most powerful particle accelerator, housed in a 27 km circumference tunnel beneath the Franco-Swiss border at CERN. It collides protons at center-of-mass energies up to $\sim 13.6$ TeV ($\sim 10^{13}$ eV), directly probing the [[Electroweak]] scale and beyond. Its crowning achievement was the 2012 discovery of the [[Higgs Boson]] at $\sim 125$ GeV by the ATLAS and CMS experiments, confirming the mechanism of electroweak symmetry breaking. The LHC also collides lead nuclei to recreate [[Quark-Gluon Plasma]] and searches for signatures of supersymmetry, extra dimensions, and other physics beyond the Standard Model.
