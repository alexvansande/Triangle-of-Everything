A hypothetical class of dark matter particles with masses in the $\sim 10$–$10^{4}$ GeV/$c^2$ range, interacting only through gravity and the weak nuclear force. For nearly four decades they were the dominant target of dark matter searches because of the **"WIMP miracle"** — a stable particle at the [[Electroweak]] scale with weak-strength annihilation naturally produces a thermal relic abundance matching the observed dark matter density, a striking coincidence that pointed toward new physics at the same energies probed by colliders. Their canonical realization is the neutralino of [[Supersymmetry]], and entire generations of underground experiments — [[XENON1T]], LUX, PandaX, SuperCDMS, DarkSide — were built around them.

Despite this, no confirmed direct-detection signal has ever been recorded, and successive experiments have driven exclusion limits orders of magnitude into the predicted parameter space, with the latest results pushing the most natural WIMP models toward the irreducible "neutrino floor." The simplest supersymmetric scenarios are now largely excluded, and attention has shifted toward much lighter candidates such as [[Axion]]s and sterile neutrinos, much heavier ones such as primordial black holes and [[Macro Dark Matter|macro dark matter]], or modifications to gravity itself.

WIMPs are therefore included here as a primarily *historical* object: increasingly unlikely to be the answer, yet so central to the design of nearly every major dark matter experiment of the last forty years that the exclusion contours they leave behind form the foundational map of what dark matter is *not* — an indispensable starting point for whatever it turns out to be.
