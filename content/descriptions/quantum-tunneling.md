The energy scale of $\sim 10^{-12}$ eV (picoelectronvolts) characteristic of ultra-slow quantum processes in which particles traverse potential energy barriers that are classically impenetrable. The tunneling probability falls exponentially with barrier width and height: $T \propto e^{-2\kappa d}$, where $\kappa = \sqrt{2m(V - E)}/\hbar$ and $d$ is the barrier thickness. This mechanism is essential across physics — it drives nuclear fusion in stellar cores (where protons tunnel through the Coulomb barrier at energies far below its peak), governs the lifetimes of radioactive alpha decay, and enables the atomic-resolution imaging of scanning tunneling microscopes. At picoelectronvolt energies, tunneling timescales can stretch to cosmological durations, connecting quantum mechanics to the very slowest processes in nature.
