A rogue planet drifting freely through interstellar space, gravitationally unbound to any host star. Current estimates suggest billions of such worlds roam the Milky Way alone — potentially outnumbering stars. Most are thought to be gas giants or super-Earths ejected from their birth systems by gravitational interactions with sibling planets or passing stars, though some may have formed independently from collapsing gas clouds too small to ignite fusion. Detection is extremely difficult: without a parent star to transit or illuminate them, rogue planets are found almost exclusively through gravitational microlensing events, in which the planet's gravity briefly magnifies the light of a background star.
