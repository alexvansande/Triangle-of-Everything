The energy scale at $\sim 100$ GeV ($\sim 10^{11}$ eV) where the electromagnetic and weak nuclear forces unify into a single electroweak interaction, described by the $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ gauge theory of Glashow, Weinberg, and Salam. Above this energy, the W$^\pm$ and Z$^0$ bosons are massless and the two forces are indistinguishable; below it, the Higgs field acquires a nonzero vacuum expectation value ($v \approx 246$ GeV), spontaneously breaking the symmetry and giving masses of $\sim 80$ GeV and $\sim 91$ GeV to the W and Z bosons respectively. This unification was spectacularly confirmed in 1983 at CERN with the discovery of the W and Z bosons, and completed in 2012 with the observation of the [[Higgs Boson]] at the [[Large Hadron Collider]].
