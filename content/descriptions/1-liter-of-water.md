One liter of pure water — one kilogram, about the size of a fist. This chart uses a sphere-equivalent radius for its horizontal axis, so a liter of water appears at the radius of a sphere containing exactly 1000 cm&sup3;: about 6.2 cm. From a [[1 mL of Water|single milliliter]] to this liter, mass spans three orders of magnitude while width spans only one — that's the power of three dimensions.
