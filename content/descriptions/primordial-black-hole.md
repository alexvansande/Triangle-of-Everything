Black holes can, in theory, be of any size, but they all require extraordinary energy and density conditions to form. Those conditions were present in the early moments of the universe, leading to speculation among physicists that some small black holes could have formed — which is why they are called primordial.

If such black holes ever formed, most of them would have already evaporated due to Hawking radiation, but some of these extraordinary objects could still be around: they weigh as much as an asteroid but are the size of a single atom.

If they exist, they would be nearly impossible to detect except through their gravitational effects, which is why they are considered candidates for explaining dark matter.
