The projected final state of the universe, expected after roughly $10^{100}$ years or more. As the cosmos continues its accelerating expansion driven by dark energy, all stars will exhaust their fuel, galaxies will disperse, and even black holes will eventually evaporate through Hawking radiation. The universe asymptotically approaches thermodynamic equilibrium — a state of maximum entropy where no gradients in temperature or energy remain to drive any process. No work can be performed, no structures can form, and no computation or life is possible. This is not a dramatic end but an eternal, featureless stillness. The heat death follows from the second law of thermodynamics applied to an ever-expanding universe with a positive cosmological constant, and represents the most widely accepted long-term fate of the cosmos among physicists today.
