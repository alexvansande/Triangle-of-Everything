One metric tonne of pure water — a cubic meter, a thousand liters. Notice that a [[Human]] is roughly the same width but much lighter: we're not solid spheres, and our average density is just under water's. The [[Hippopotamus]] on the other hand is denser than water — they can walk along river bottoms! From a [[1 mL of Water|single milliliter]] to this tonne, mass spans six orders of magnitude while width spans only two.
