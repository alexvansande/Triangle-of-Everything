Hypothetical gauge bosons predicted by [[Grand Unification Theory]] models such as $SU(5)$ and $SO(10)$, with estimated masses of $\sim 10^{15}$ GeV/$c^2$ — near the GUT scale. X and Y bosons would mediate direct interactions between quarks and leptons, processes forbidden in the Standard Model, most notably enabling proton decay with a predicted lifetime of $\sim 10^{34}$–$10^{36}$ years. Their existence would confirm that the strong, weak, and electromagnetic forces unify into a single gauge interaction at extremely high energies. No X or Y boson has ever been observed, and the failure to detect proton decay in experiments like Super-Kamiokande has already ruled out the simplest GUT models.
