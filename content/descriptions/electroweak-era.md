The period from $\sim 10^{-32}$ s to $\sim 10^{-12}$ s after the [[Big Bang]], when the strong force has already separated but the electromagnetic and weak nuclear forces remain unified as the [[Electroweak]] interaction. The universe is a dense plasma of quarks, leptons, and gauge bosons at temperatures above $\sim 10^{15}$ K. This era ends with electroweak symmetry breaking at $\sim 100$ GeV, when the [[Higgs Boson|Higgs field]] acquires its vacuum expectation value. The W$^\pm$ and Z$^0$ bosons gain mass, the electromagnetic and weak forces become distinct, and fundamental particles acquire their masses through the Higgs mechanism. This phase transition is the highest-energy process confirmed by experiment at the [[Large Hadron Collider]].
