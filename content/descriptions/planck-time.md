The Planck time is the natural unit of time when h, c, G, and τ are set to 1. It is approximately 5.39×10⁻⁴⁴ seconds—the time it takes light to cross a Planck length. It marks the earliest meaningful moment in conventional cosmology.

Formula (using h and τ): t_P = √(hG/(τc⁵))

Together with the Planck length, mass, and energy, it completes the set of fundamental Planck units in a consistent h-and-τ formulation.
