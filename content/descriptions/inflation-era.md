A brief but transformative period from roughly $10^{-36}$ s to $10^{-32}$ s after the [[Big Bang]], during which the universe expanded exponentially — increasing in volume by a factor of at least $10^{78}$. Proposed by Alan Guth in 1981, inflation solves several cosmological puzzles: why the universe appears spatially flat (the flatness problem), why distant regions of the sky have nearly identical temperatures (the horizon problem), and why magnetic monopoles predicted by GUTs are not observed. Quantum fluctuations stretched to cosmic scales during inflation became the seeds of all large-scale structure — galaxies, clusters, and the anisotropies in the [[CMB Photon|cosmic microwave background]]. Inflation ends with a process called reheating, which fills the universe with the hot particle soup of the [[Electroweak Era|ELECTROWEAK EPOCH]].
