A liter of water shaped as a sphere, about 12.4 cm across. It plots almost identically to the [[1 Liter of Water (cube)|cube version]] — proof that on a logarithmic chart, geometry is irrelevant. This is why we can plot everything from [[Electron|electrons]] to [[Observable Universe|the observable universe]] on the same axes without worrying about whether something is round, flat, or oddly shaped.
