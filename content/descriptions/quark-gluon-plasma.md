A primordial soup of deconfined quarks and gluons, existing as free particles rather than being bound inside hadrons. This exotic state of matter filled the universe during the first microseconds after the [[Big Bang]], at temperatures exceeding $\sim 2 \times 10^{12}$ K ($\sim 170$ MeV). At these temperatures, the strong force becomes too weak to confine quarks — a phenomenon known as asymptotic freedom. Quark-gluon plasma behaves as a nearly perfect liquid with extremely low viscosity, as demonstrated by heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and the [[Large Hadron Collider]], where lead nuclei are smashed together to briefly recreate this primordial state.
