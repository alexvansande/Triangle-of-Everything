Iceberg A-23a: calved from Antarctica's Filchner Ice Shelf in 1986 and remained grounded on the seafloor for 37 years before drifting free around 2020. At its peak it spanned roughly 4,000 km² — twice the size of Greater London — and weighed nearly a trillion tonnes, making it the world's largest iceberg. By early 2026 meltwater had turned its surface a vivid blue as hydrofracturing tore it apart, a dramatic end to a four-decade journey. Its mass rivals that of a small mountain, placing it in the same neighborhood as Mt. Everest on this chart.
