The energy scale of $\sim 1$ GeV ($10^{9}$ eV) marks the QCD confinement threshold, where the strong force becomes powerful enough to permanently bind quarks and gluons into hadrons — baryons (like protons and neutrons) and mesons (like pions). Below this energy, quarks cannot exist as free particles; the coupling constant $\alpha_s$ grows so large that any attempt to separate quarks produces new quark–antiquark pairs from the vacuum. The proton mass, $m_p \approx 938$ MeV/$c^2$, sets this characteristic scale, with most of it arising not from the bare quark masses but from the kinetic and binding energy of the strong field itself. This is the domain explored by the [[Large Hadron Collider]] and earlier colliders that mapped out the particle zoo of the Standard Model.
