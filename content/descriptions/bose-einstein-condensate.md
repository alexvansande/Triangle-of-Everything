The energy scale of $\sim 10^{-9}$ eV (nanoelectronvolts) at which ultracold bosonic atoms undergo a phase transition into a single macroscopic quantum state, with all particles occupying the ground state of the confining potential. First achieved experimentally in 1995 by Eric Cornell and Carl Wieman using rubidium-87 atoms cooled to $\sim 170$ nanokelvin, this state of matter was predicted by Satyendra Nath Bose and Albert Einstein in 1924–1925. In a BEC, the thermal de Broglie wavelength $\lambda_{\mathrm{dB}} = h / \sqrt{2\pi m k_B T}$ exceeds the interparticle spacing, causing individual atomic wave functions to overlap and merge into a coherent matter wave. This makes quantum phenomena — such as interference and superfluidity — directly observable at macroscopic scales.
