The Eridanus Supervoid is associated with the "Cold Spot"—an anomalous region in the cosmic microwave background that is significantly colder than the surrounding sky. This supervoid may span up to 1 billion light-years across, and some theories suggest it could explain the Cold Spot through the integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect. The existence and significance of the Eridanus Supervoid remain topics of active research and debate.
