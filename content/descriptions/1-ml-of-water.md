A milliliter of pure water — one cubic centimeter, one gram. Water's density of exactly 1 g/cm&sup3; originally defined the gram itself, making it the universal reference for mass and volume. On this chart, all three water references (1 mL, [[1 Liter of Water|1 liter]], [[1 Tonne of Water|1 tonne]]) sit on the water density line, serving as anchor points for the entire scale. Notice how nearby [[Water (H₂O)|water molecules]] also fall close to this line!
