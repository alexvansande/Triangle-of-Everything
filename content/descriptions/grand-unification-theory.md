A theoretical framework proposing that the strong, weak, and electromagnetic forces merge into a single unified gauge interaction at energies of $\sim 10^{25}$ eV ($\sim 10^{16}$ GeV), corresponding to length scales of $\sim 10^{-32}$ cm. Candidate gauge groups include $SU(5)$, $SO(10)$, and $E_6$, each predicting that the three Standard Model coupling constants converge to a single value at the GUT scale. A key consequence is proton decay, mediated by superheavy [[X Boson]] gauge bosons, along with potential explanations for the matter–antimatter asymmetry of the universe. While the running of coupling constants in the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) achieves near-exact unification at $\sim 2 \times 10^{16}$ GeV, direct experimental evidence for grand unification remains elusive.
