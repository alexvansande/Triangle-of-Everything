The star at the center of our Solar System — a G-type main-sequence star (G2V) about 4.6 billion years old. It contains 99.86% of the Solar System's total mass and fuses roughly 600 million tonnes of hydrogen per second in its core. The energy it radiates sustains life on [[Earth]] and drives our planet's climate and weather.
