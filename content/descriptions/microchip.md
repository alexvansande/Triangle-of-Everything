A modern microprocessor die: roughly 1 cm across and thinner than a fingernail, yet etched with billions of transistors at features as small as 3 nanometers — just a dozen atoms wide. It sits on this chart at about the same size as a ladybug, but represents perhaps the most complex single object humans have ever manufactured. The smallest transistor ever demonstrated has a 1-nanometer gate, placing engineered precision firmly in the realm of individual molecules.
